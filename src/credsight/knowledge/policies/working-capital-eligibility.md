# Working Capital — Unsecured Small-Ticket Credit Policy (synthetic stand-in)

> Synthetic policy document for the CredSight build. Stands in for the bank's real
> credit-policy PDF until sandbox access is granted. Clauses are illustrative.

## Eligible segment
Unsecured working-capital credit is offered to micro and small enterprises (MSMEs),
including New-To-Credit (NTC) and New-To-Bank (NTB) borrowers in retail, services, and
micro-manufacturing. Formal audited financials are not mandatory where alternate data
(GST, bank/AA, UPI) is sufficient.

## Unsecured limit by score band
The indicative unsecured working-capital limit is set by the composite health-score band:
a strong band (>= 750) supports up to the standard small-ticket ceiling; a good band
(680–749) a reduced ceiling; a fair band (600–679) a micro ceiling. Below 600 the
application is referred for manual review rather than auto-offered.

## Pricing and tenor
Indicative interest rate is risk-based and increases as the score band falls. Tenor is
capped shorter for lower bands and for thin-file borrowers. Final rate and tenor are
confirmed at sanction.

## Turnover corroboration
Declared turnover should corroborate across GST returns and bank inflows within tolerance.
A material unexplained gap reduces confidence and routes the case to manual review.

## Obligation load
Existing obligation servicing (EMI/inflow) must remain within prudential limits. High
leverage relative to inflow lowers the eligible limit or refers the case for review.
