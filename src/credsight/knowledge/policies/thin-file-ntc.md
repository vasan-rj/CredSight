# Thin-File / New-To-Credit Assessment Guidance (synthetic stand-in)

> Synthetic guidance for assessing credit-invisible MSMEs on alternate data.

## Thin-file definition
A thin-file applicant has limited credit history and/or few data sources present. Such
cases carry a lower confidence indicator and must be surfaced honestly rather than scored
with false precision.

## Confidence and the human gate
When the thin-file confidence falls below the configured floor, the case is routed to a
human underwriter regardless of the score. Low data is a reason for review, not automatic
rejection.

## Alternate data sufficiency
For NTC micro-enterprises, regular UPI inflows, punctual GST filing, and stable bank
balances can establish creditworthiness in the absence of a bureau record. Absence of a
bureau record alone is not grounds for rejection.

## Seasonality
Seasonal businesses show expected periodic variation in turnover and inflows. Seasonality
should be recognised and not penalised as instability.
